What is Local Nature Recovery for?

Part of the Environmental Land Management scheme, Local Nature Recovery will introduce a new approach to paying for public goods with:

  • more choice and control for farmers
  • a greater focus on outcomes and the main practices that support them
  • a fairer, more supportive and effective approach to land management

What is included?

We will pay for delivery of land management actions that contribute to:

  • clean air
  • clean and plentiful water
  • thriving plants and wildlife
  • reduction in and protection from environmental hazards
  • mitigation of and adaptation to climate change
  • enhanced beauty, heritage and engagement with the environment

How does the Local Nature Recovery scheme work?

Local Nature Recovery will pay for actions that deliver environmental benefits.

You can add separate options to your application that offer improvements such as in overall air quality or wildlife habitation.

Local Nature Recovery is a competitive process. This means that applications containing components marked as 'Local Priority' are more likely to be successful than other, lower priority bids.

How will you pay for public goods?

The way we pay for public goods will be different with less prescription, more choice and greater flexibility.

The length of the agreements will be flexible according to individual circumstances and the local nature benefits you want to deliver.
